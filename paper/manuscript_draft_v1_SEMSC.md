## Abstract

Multilayer thin-film encapsulation (TFE) is a gating technology for moisture-sensitive photovoltaics, yet its geometric design space—organic/inorganic layer thicknesses and the number of dyads—is still explored experimentally at only a handful of points. We report a lightweight, physics-informed digital twin that treats TFE design as a geometry problem: one-dimensional moisture transport in water-activity form with defect-mediated effective media (nucleation-driven pinhole closure, a particle-defect floor, a channel-cracking penalty, and lateral tortuous-path decoupling), coupled heat transfer, and an exact layer-lumped time-lag. The transport core is verified against the analytic Fickian solution (0.45% maximum flux error). Calibrated only on a published single-layer ALD Al₂O₃ thickness series and the one-dyad point of the same source [7], the twin predicts two- and three-dyad water vapor transmission rates with zero refitting to within +0.02 and −0.24 decades once the finite (~72 h, source-confirmed) test duration is simulated; steady-state theory alone cannot reproduce the observed per-dyad improvement under any admissible parameters, identifying the measurements as lag-phase-limited and explaining sub-detection-limit data points. The inferred pinhole density (5.1 mm⁻²; 444 µm spacing) falls within independent Ca-test literature ranges. Exhaustive enumeration of 4,104 designs provides a ground-truth six-objective Pareto front that a dependency-free NSGA-II reproduces (generational distance 0.060), and reveals an effective dimensionality reduction: device temperature and transmittance vary by only 0.10 K and 0.35 percentage points across the space. Exact interventional Shapley values and Sobol indices computed on the factorial grid—no surrogate approximation—are translated into causal chains in which every arrow carries model-internal quantities; this number-verified interpretation autonomously detected and corrected an over-interpretation of the organic-thickness effect. Monte-Carlo uncertainty propagation through the entire calibration pipeline (N = 500) yields confidence-qualified design rules: an inorganic-thickness design window of 22.5 [21.0–26.0] to 44.0 [37.7–48.0] nm bounded by pinhole closure and cracking; a lifetime pair law that is linear without saturation in all 500 draws (R² ≥ 0.9998), with each dyad adding 0.5 [0.4–0.5] of the three-dyad lifetime; and an explicit separation between structurally robust conclusions and absolute lifetimes that await calibration of the critical moisture dose. The framework, database, and code are released to enable geometry-first encapsulation design.

## 1. Introduction

Perovskite photovoltaics have reached efficiencies competitive with silicon, but their intrinsic sensitivity to moisture makes encapsulation a decisive—and still unsettled—step toward deployment; recent reviews emphasize both the dual requirement of blocking water ingress and lead egress and the absence of standardized stability protocols [1,2]. Among encapsulation strategies, multilayer thin-film encapsulation (TFE), alternating dense inorganic barriers with organic decoupling layers, offers conformality, flexibility and low thermal budget compatible with perovskite devices [3–7].

Experimentally, the TFE literature has established the key ingredients. Low-temperature ALD Al₂O₃ single layers protect devices but plateau in barrier performance [4–6]; organic/inorganic dyads improve the water vapor transmission rate (WVTR) by orders of magnitude, with a 3-dyad pV3D3/Al₂O₃ stack reaching ~10⁻⁴ g m⁻² day⁻¹ at 38 °C/90% RH [3] and a 3-dyad parylene-C/Al₂O₃ stack falling below instrument detection [7]; and residual particles introduced during deposition, rather than intrinsic film quality, dominate the defect budget of practical barriers [7]. Yet these studies sample the geometric design space—organic thickness d_org, inorganic thickness d_in, dyad number n—at only a few points each, and the recent proposal of a multi-property figure of merit for TFE [15] confirms the community's recognition that barrier, optical and mechanical demands must be balanced, while still collapsing them into a single scalar ranking of fabricated films.

The transport physics required to map this space has long been available. Graff et al. showed that multilayer barrier performance is governed by the lag time associated with long lateral diffusion paths between laterally offset defects rather than by equilibrium permeation [8]; ideal-laminate resistance models and analytic lag-time expressions reproduce time-dependent permeation through multilayers [9]; and moisture-ingress modeling is established at the PV-module scale [10]. These models, however, have served as analysis tools for fabricated structures—isothermal, mostly steady-state—rather than as forward engines coupled to environmental dynamics, thermo-mechanical durability, lifetime prediction, and systematic design-space search.

Machine learning has entered adjacent problems from a different direction. Random-forest models with SHAP attribution identified molecular descriptors governing capping-layer stabilization of perovskites [11]; NSGA-II has optimized layer thicknesses of solar cells for optoelectronic objectives [12,13]; and an AutoML pipeline for perovskite processing has been described as an early component of a device digital twin [14]. None of these targets encapsulation geometry, couples a transport PDE to the optimizer, treats reliability-centered objectives, or grounds the statistical attribution in transport mechanisms; SHAP importances, in particular, answer *what mattered* but not *why*.

Here we assemble these validated pieces into a single framework and ask what the assembly newly enables. Specifically we contribute: (i) a lightweight (≈1 s per design) defect-mediated 1-D moisture/heat digital twin whose geometry sensitivity derives from explicit pinhole-closure, particle-floor, cracking and tortuous-path physics; (ii) a disclosed calibration/validation protocol on literature data—shape from a single-layer thickness series, absolute scale from one multilayer point, and zero-refit prediction of the remaining multilayer points—including a transient re-interpretation of sub-detection-limit measurements; (iii) a six-objective Pareto exploration verified against exhaustive enumeration, with exact (not surrogate-approximated) Shapley and Sobol attribution translated into causal chains that carry model-internal numbers and are therefore self-verifying; and (iv) design rules qualified by Monte-Carlo uncertainty propagated through the calibration pipeline itself. We claim no new material, transport law, or optimization algorithm; the contribution is the validated integration and the design map and rules it produces.

## 2. Model and methods

### 2.1 Governing model
Moisture transport is solved in water-activity form, S(x) ∂a/∂t = ∂x(P(x,T) ∂x a), where a = C/S is continuous across interfaces (continuity of chemical potential) and equals the relative humidity at the ambient boundary; the device acts as a perfect sink (a = 0), the worst case corresponding to Ca-type tests. Temperature enters through Arrhenius diffusivities. Geometry sensitivity is carried by defect-mediated effective media: for the inorganic layers, P_in^eff = P_lat(T) + f(d) P_org(T) with

f(d) = f₀ e^(−d/d_c) + f_res + f_c0[(d − d_crit)/d_crit]₊²,

encoding nucleation-driven pinhole closure, a thickness-independent particle floor [7], and a channel-cracking penalty; for sandwiched organic layers, P_org^eff = P_org/τ² with τ² = 1 + s² ln(s/r)/(2π d_org²) and s = r√(π/f), the one-dimensional reduction of the lateral detour between laterally offset pinholes [8]. Heat conduction with a solar source, convective and sky-radiative boundaries supplies T(t) for Arrhenius coupling and the thermal-cycling amplitude used in a Basquin fatigue proxy with thickness-dependent channel-cracking strength σ_c(d) = σ_c0 √(d_ref/d). Lifetime is a cumulative-dose model, T80 = t_lag + M_crit R_tot/Δa, with the exact layer-lumped Frisch lag t_lag = Σ_j C_j R_L,j R_R,j / R_tot (which reduces to L²/6D for a uniform slab). The finite-volume, harmonic-conductance, fully implicit solver reproduces the analytic Fickian breakthrough flux to 0.45% maximum error (Fig. 2; Supplementary code).

### 2.2 Literature database and provenance
All inputs live in a relational CSV database with per-row source identifiers, measurement conditions, censoring flags (=, <, ~), quality grades (A: primary and verified; B: primary, bibliography pending; C: handbook/assumed), and an enforced calibration/validation role split. Graphical data were digitized with an automated marker-detection pipeline whose axis calibration and overlay verification are archived (residuals ≤ 0.14 decade; point uncertainty ±0.15 decade).

### 2.3 Calibration and zero-refit validation
Stage A fits the shape of f(d) to the single-layer ALD Al₂O₃ thickness series of Ref. [7] (15–60 nm at 38 °C/100% RH; maximum residual 0.047 decade), under the assumption—shown self-consistent a posteriori (bare-pinhole conductance 5.8×10⁻⁶ of the open-vapor limit, i.e., access-limited)—that bare-layer WVTR is proportional to f(d). The falling branch is constrained by a single datum, leaving (f₀, d_c) on an identifiability ridge; accordingly only d ≥ 15 nm is admitted to the design space. Stage B sets the absolute scale f_res from the one-dyad point of the same source. The two- and three-dyad measurements are then *predicted with zero refitting*, both at steady state and as apparent WVTR after the finite test duration of the coulometric instrument (≈72 h, confirmed from the source). The within-source split (single layers + one multilayer point for calibration; remaining multilayer points for validation) is disclosed as such; an independent-source check against a 50-nm film measured at 45 °C [4] and the qualitative low-quality-film regime of Ref. [7] complement it.

### 2.4 Six-objective exploration
Design variables are d_org ∈ [100, 1000] nm (lower bound a planarization/process constraint; see §3.5), d_in ∈ [15, 120] nm, n ∈ {1…6}. Objectives: T80 at 38 °C/90% RH; peak device temperature under a diurnal cycle (15–42 °C, 1000 W m⁻² peak); visible transmittance (incoherent Fresnel + Beer–Lambert); a cost proxy equal to measured deposition time (ALD 0.098 nm min⁻¹ vs. parylene 6 nm min⁻¹ [7]); areal mass; and the fatigue-durability proxy. The analytic evaluator is machine-precision-equal to the PDE solver at steady state and exact in its lag term, enabling exhaustive enumeration of 4,104 designs as a Pareto ground truth against which a dependency-free NSGA-II (population 80, 80 generations, SBX/polynomial operators) is verified.

### 2.5 Exact explainability and causal chains
Because the design grid is full-factorial, interventional Shapley values are computed exactly from the 2³ coalition group means (efficiency identity satisfied to ≤7×10⁻¹² of the response), and Sobol variance shares follow from exact functional-ANOVA—no surrogate model or sampling approximation. Each high-importance (driver → objective) pair is then rendered as a causal chain whose every arrow carries model-internal quantities (f, s, τ², R_in, R_sand, t_lag, σ_c/σ_a) recomputed from probes, together with a regression check of the claimed law.

### 2.6 Uncertainty quantification through the calibration pipeline
N = 500 Monte-Carlo draws perturb the measured inputs (single-layer series, log-σ = 0.08 decade; digitized anchor, 0.10 decade) and the placeholder properties (parylene D ×/÷3 and S within the validation-constrained 0.5–2× range; Ea 30–50 kJ mol⁻¹; pinhole radius ×/÷2; M_crit ×/÷3; σ_c0 ×/÷1.5; fatigue exponent 6–10), and the *entire Stage A/B calibration is re-run per draw*, so calibration-induced parameter correlations (e.g., f_res anti-correlated with parylene permeability through the scale anchor) are preserved automatically.

## 3. Results and discussion

### 3.1 Calibrated pinhole physics and a transient re-reading of multilayer WVTR
The fitted single-layer curve (Fig. 3a) reproduces the non-monotonic thickness dependence—closure to a defect floor by ~20 nm, degradation beyond ~50 nm—of Ref. [7], and the absolute scale implies a pinhole density of 5.1 mm⁻² (spacing 444 µm), squarely within Ca-test decoration ranges reported for good ALD alumina, a plausibility check independent of the fit. Zero-refit steady-state prediction of the dyad series, however, fails structurally: the measured per-dyad improvement is geometric (÷4.7 per dyad), whereas any series-resistance ladder of this model class saturates arithmetically, overshooting the 3-dyad point by +0.69 decade under all admissible parameters. Simulating the finite, source-confirmed ≈72 h test resolves the discrepancy quantitatively (Fig. 3b,c): predicted apparent WVTR matches the 2-dyad point to +0.02 decade and the 3-dyad point to −0.24 decade, with lag times of 74 h and 203 h respectively. This identifies the multilayer measurements as lag-phase-limited, explains why plotted values fall below the instrument's stated steady-state detection limit, and reconciles the apparent geometric scaling with series-resistance physics—an argument only a time-domain twin can make. The parylene solubility required for this joint agreement (0.5–2 kg m⁻³) coincides with the independent datasheet uptake (<0.1 wt%), closing a non-circular consistency loop.

### 3.2 Pareto structure and effective dimensionality
Across all 4,104 designs the peak device temperature spans only 0.10 K and the transmittance 0.35 percentage points: TFE nanolayers add ~mK conduction resistance against ~10 W m⁻² K⁻¹ boundary losses, and parylene (n = 1.639) is index-matched to Al₂O₃ (n = 1.65). The nominally six-objective problem therefore collapses to an effective four-objective one (lifetime, cost, weight, durability; 247 designs on the true reduced front), itself a design insight: in this material pair, thermal and optical performance are not geometric degrees of freedom and should be addressed at the materials-selection stage. The self-implemented NSGA-II lands on the exhaustive front (generational distance 0.060 mean; the larger inverted GD of 0.168 reflects an 80-point approximation of a 3,064-point six-objective front, i.e., cardinality rather than convergence). Lifetime climbs the dyad ladder linearly (+8,150–8,350 h per pair at baseline parameters) with no in-model saturation, so the pair count is set by cost/weight constraints rather than by diminishing physical returns; representative designs are listed in Table 2.

### 3.3 Number-verified causal chains, and a self-correction
Exact Sobol shares (Fig. 5a) place d_in first for lifetime (0.551), followed by the d_in×n interaction (0.184)—structurally expected, since the dominant resistance is the product n·R_in(d_in), and physically resonant with the collapse of dyad benefits for particle-rich films in Ref. [7] ("poor barriers cannot be stacked into good ones"). Durability loads entirely on d_in (1.00) through σ_c(d) ∝ d^(−1/2), giving N_f ∝ d^(−m/2) (measured log–log slope −3.6 under the fatigue prior). The chain rendering proved more than presentational: embedding model internals into the n = 1 organic-thickness chain exposed that R_top is seven orders below R_in, so d_org is inert for single dyads; our own earlier reading of the optimizer's boundary choice ("thick organic helps at n = 1") was a tie-breaking artifact, and the published-format chain corrected it. The organic layer contributes to lifetime *only* as the lateral decoupling medium of multilayers (R_sand ∝ 1/d_org once n ≥ 2), a sign-changing, magnitude-limited effect (Fig. 5c, 6).

### 3.4 Robust design rules under calibration-through uncertainty
Propagating all input uncertainties through re-calibration (Fig. 7) yields rules with explicit confidence: **(G1)** inorganic thickness at least 22.5 [21.0–26.0] nm (pinhole closure); **(G2)** below 44.0 [37.7–48.0] nm (cracking onset; beyond it lifetime and durability collapse jointly and designs are Pareto-dominated); **(G3)** within this window the lifetime–durability exchange follows N_f ∝ d^(−m/2), and a 10-year fatigue target requires d_in ≤ 30 [16–49] nm while being unreachable at any thickness in 16% of draws under the fatigue prior—an honest quantification of the durability proxy's weakness and a target for primary-literature reinforcement; **(G4)** the pair law is linear in every one of 500 draws (R² ≥ 0.9998), with a tight relative gain of 0.5 [0.4–0.5] of the three-dyad lifetime per added dyad, while the absolute gain (7,592 [1,321–45,276] h) is dominated by the M_crit prior—structure is settled, absolute lifetime awaits dose calibration; **(G5)** organic layers at the process-minimum thickness (here ≥100 nm); **(G6)** thermal/optical objectives delegated to materials selection; **(G7)** thresholds and rankings are invariant under M_crit ×/÷3. The robust-membership map (Fig. 7c) condenses these into a fabrication-ready chart.

### 3.5 Limitations and transferability
(i) The organic-thickness optimum sits on the imposed 100 nm process bound because pinhole replication through thin organics is not modeled; the bound is a planarization constraint, not a physics result. (ii) Absolute lifetimes carry a ×34 credible span driven by M_crit; transferring to perovskite devices requires calibrating M_crit against encapsulated-device aging (e.g., the 7,500 h shelf data of Ref. [4]). (iii) The cracking onset was calibrated under flexible-substrate handling strain [7]; rigid or unstrained deployments shift d_crit upward and require a regime flag. (iv) The durability channel is a Basquin proxy pending film-mechanics primary data. (v) The calibration/validation split is within one source, mitigated by cross-regime (single-layer → multilayer) prediction, an independent cross-temperature check [4], and the constrained-solubility consistency; a fully independent dyad dataset (e.g., Ref. [3], Fig. 2d) is the highest-value future validation. (vi) Lifetime rankings are evaluated at 38 °C/90% RH; Arrhenius extrapolation rescales absolute values without reordering geometry.

## 4. Conclusions
Treating thin-film encapsulation as a geometry problem, a literature-calibrated, defect-mediated digital twin—verified against analytic transport, validated zero-refit against multilayer experiments including a transient re-interpretation of sub-detection-limit data, explored to an exhaustively verified Pareto front, explained through exact and self-verifying causal chains, and stress-tested by calibration-through uncertainty propagation—yields a compact set of confidence-qualified design rules, chief among them a 22.5–44 nm inorganic design window and a saturation-free linear pair law. The framework is lightweight, dependency-minimal, and released with its database and provenance; its most consequential open items are the critical moisture dose of perovskite absorbers and primary film-mechanics data, both of which slot directly into the released pipeline.

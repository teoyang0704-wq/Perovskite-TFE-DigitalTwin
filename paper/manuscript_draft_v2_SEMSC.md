## Title
Geometry-first design of organic/inorganic multilayer thin-film encapsulation via a literature-calibrated digital twin: defect-mediated moisture physics, zero-refit validation, and confidence-qualified design rules

## Abstract

Multilayer thin-film encapsulation (TFE) gates the deployment of moisture-sensitive optoelectronics, including perovskite photovoltaics, yet its geometric design space—organic/inorganic thicknesses and dyad number—is sampled experimentally at only a few points. We report a lightweight digital twin that treats TFE design as a geometry problem: one-dimensional moisture transport in water-activity form with defect-mediated effective media (nucleation-driven pinhole closure, a particle floor, a cracking penalty, and lateral tortuous-path decoupling), coupled heat transfer, and an exact layer-lumped time lag; the transport core is verified against the analytic Fickian solution (0.45% maximum error). The framework is demonstrated end-to-end on the ALD-Al₂O₃/parylene-C literature system: calibrated only on a published single-layer thickness series plus the one-dyad point of the same source, it predicts the two- and three-dyad water vapor transmission rates with zero refitting to within +0.02 and −0.24 decades once the finite (≈72 h, source-stated) test duration is simulated—steady-state theory provably cannot reproduce the observed per-dyad improvement—thereby identifying the measurements as lag-phase-limited and remaining consistent with their below-detection-limit (censored) status. The inferred pinhole density (5.1 mm⁻²) falls within independent Ca-test ranges. Exhaustive enumeration of 4,104 designs supplies a ground-truth six-objective Pareto front, against which a dependency-free NSGA-II—retained as verified machinery for higher-dimensional extensions—is benchmarked (generational distance 0.060); device temperature and transmittance vary by only 0.10 K and 0.35 percentage points, collapsing the problem to four effective objectives. Exact interventional Shapley values and Sobol indices on the factorial grid are translated into causal chains carrying model-internal quantities; this number-embedding convention exposed and corrected an over-interpretation of the organic-thickness effect. Monte-Carlo uncertainty propagated through the entire calibration pipeline (N = 500) yields confidence-qualified rules: an inorganic design window of 22.5 [21.0–26.0]–44.0 [37.7–48.0] nm bounded by pinhole closure and cracking, and a lifetime pair law linear in all draws (R² ≥ 0.9998). Structural conclusions are robust; absolute lifetimes await calibration of the perovskite critical moisture dose, the framework's stated transfer requirement. Code, database and provenance are released.

## 1. Introduction

Perovskite photovoltaics now rival silicon in efficiency, but moisture sensitivity makes encapsulation a decisive step toward deployment; recent reviews stress the dual requirement of blocking water ingress and lead egress and the absence of standardized stability protocols [1,2]. Among encapsulation routes, multilayer thin-film encapsulation—alternating dense inorganic barriers with organic interlayers—offers conformality, flexibility and a low thermal budget compatible with perovskite devices [3–7].

Experiment has established the ingredients. Low-temperature ALD Al₂O₃ single layers protect devices but plateau in barrier performance [4–6], consistent with the ALD-barrier benchmarks of Carcia et al. [19]; organic/inorganic dyads improve the water vapor transmission rate (WVTR) by orders of magnitude, with a 3-dyad pV3D3/Al₂O₃ stack near 10⁻⁴ g m⁻² day⁻¹ at 38 °C/90% RH [3] and a 3-dyad parylene-C/Al₂O₃ stack below instrument detection [7]; ALD nanolaminates extend the same logic within the inorganic phase [20]; and residual particles introduced during deposition, rather than intrinsic film quality, dominate the defect budget of practical barriers [7]—particles whose burial is a principal *planarization* function of the organic layers [21]. Yet these studies sample the geometry (d_org, d_in, n) at a handful of points each, and the recent multi-property figure of merit for TFE [15] confirms that barrier, optical and mechanical demands must be balanced while still collapsing them into a single scalar ranking of fabricated films.

The transport physics needed to map this space is long established. Permeation through coated polymers is defect-dominated: single-pinhole models [17] and defect–permeation correlations on PECVD-coated PET [18] quantified it two decades ago; Graff et al. showed that multilayer performance is governed by the *lag time* of long lateral paths between offset defects rather than by equilibrium permeation [8]; ideal-laminate resistance models reproduce time-dependent multilayer permeation [9]; and moisture ingress is modeled at PV-module scale [10]. These tools, however, have served as isothermal, mostly steady-state analyses of fabricated structures—not as forward engines coupled to environmental dynamics, durability, lifetime, and systematic design-space search.

Machine learning has entered adjacent problems from another direction: random-forest/SHAP identification of stabilizing capping-layer chemistry [11], NSGA-II thickness optimization for optoelectronic objectives [12,13], and an AutoML processing pipeline described as an early perovskite digital-twin component [14]. None targets encapsulation geometry, couples a transport PDE to the search, treats reliability-centered objectives, or grounds attribution in mechanism—SHAP answers *what mattered*, not *why*.

Here we assemble these validated pieces and ask what the assembly newly enables, on a deliberately scoped claim: a *barrier-stack* digital twin, demonstrated and validated end-to-end on the Al₂O₃/parylene-C system of Ref. [7], *toward* perovskite encapsulation (whose device-specific transfer requirement—the critical moisture dose—we identify explicitly). Contributions: (i) a ≈1 s-per-design defect-mediated 1-D moisture/heat twin whose geometry sensitivity derives from explicit closure, particle-floor, cracking and tortuous-path physics [8,17,18]; (ii) a disclosed calibration/validation protocol—shape from a single-layer series, scale from one multilayer point, zero-refit prediction of the rest—including a transient, censoring-consistent re-reading of sub-detection measurements; (iii) a six-objective Pareto exploration verified against exhaustive enumeration, with exact Shapley/Sobol attribution rendered as causal chains that carry model-internal numbers; and (iv) design rules qualified by Monte-Carlo uncertainty propagated through the calibration pipeline itself. We claim no new material, transport law, or algorithm; the contribution is the validated integration and the design map and rules it produces.

## 2. Model and methods

### 2.1 Governing model
Moisture transport is solved in water-activity form, S(x) ∂a/∂t = ∂x(P(x,T) ∂x a), a = C/S continuous across interfaces and equal to RH at the ambient boundary; sorption is treated as activity-linear (Henry regime), an assumption revisited in §3.5. The device boundary is a perfect sink (a = 0), matching Ca/MOCON test geometry [7,17]; for barriers deposited directly on devices vapor can accumulate beneath pinholes and reduce the driving force [17], so the sink assumption is *conservative* for lifetime. Geometry sensitivity is carried by defect-mediated effective media: P_in^eff = P_lat(T) + f(d) P_org(T), with

f(d) = f₀ e^(−d/d_c) + f_res + f_c0[(d − d_crit)/d_crit]₊²,

encoding nucleation-driven closure, a thickness-independent particle floor [7,18], and channel cracking; sandwiched organic layers carry P_org^eff = P_org/τ², τ² = 1 + s² ln(s/r)/(2π d_org²), s = r√(π/f)—the 1-D reduction of the lateral detour between offset pinholes [8], valid for s ≫ d_org (here s ≈ 444 µm against d_org ≤ 1 µm; derivation in SI S3). Heat conduction with solar source and convective/sky-radiative boundaries supplies T(t) for Arrhenius coupling and the cycling amplitude of a Basquin fatigue proxy with σ_c(d) = σ_c0 √(d_ref/d). The moisture-limited lifetime is a dose-threshold proxy, t₈₀^dose = t_lag + M_crit R_tot/Δa (written T80 in figures; it maps onto the photovoltaic T80 metric only after device-level dose calibration), with the exact layer-lumped Frisch time lag [22] t_lag = Σ_j C_j R_L,j R_R,j / R_tot (reducing to L²/6D for a uniform slab; derivation SI S1). The finite-volume, harmonic-conductance, implicit solver reproduces the analytic Fickian breakthrough flux to 0.45% (Fig. 2).

### 2.2 Literature database and provenance
All inputs live in a relational database with per-row sources, conditions, censoring flags (=, <, ~), quality grades, and an enforced calibration/validation role split. Graphical data were digitized by automated marker detection with archived axis-calibration overlays (residuals ≤ 0.14 decade; ±0.15 decade per point).

### 2.3 Calibration and zero-refit validation
Stage A fits the *shape* of f(d) to the single-layer Al₂O₃ series of Ref. [7] (15–60 nm, 38 °C/100% RH; max residual 0.047 decade) under the a-posteriori-consistent assumption that bare-layer WVTR ∝ f(d) (bare-pinhole conductance 5.8×10⁻⁶ of the open-vapor limit, i.e., access-limited [17,18]). The falling branch is constrained by one datum, leaving d_c only upper-bounded (≤1.8 nm at the measurement-noise level, with f₀ compensating; profile analysis SI S3) — closure completes below the first measured thickness — so *no statement is extrapolated below 15 nm* and the design space is bounded there. Stage B sets f_res from the one-dyad point of the same source. The two- and three-dyad measurements are then *predicted with zero refitting*—at steady state and as apparent WVTR after the instrument's finite run time (≈72 h as stated in the Experimental section/SI of Ref. [7] [VERIFY exact locus]); because both points lie below the stated steady-state detection limit they are treated as *censored*, and validation is assessed as consistency under censoring plus descriptive decade errors. Duration sensitivity: assumed runs of 2.6–7.2 days keep both predictions within ≤0.4 decade (SI Fig. S3) of the plotted values. The within-source split is disclosed; complementary checks are an independent cross-temperature point (50 nm at 45 °C [4]), the collapsed-benefit regime of particle-rich films [7], and the solubility consistency of §3.1. Falsification targets for future data: the 1–4-dyad WVTR *and lag* series of Ref. [3] (Fig. 2d) and any ≤10 nm ALD point.

### 2.4 Six-objective exploration
Design variables: d_org ∈ [100, 1000] nm (planarization/process bound; §3.5), d_in ∈ [15, 120] nm, n ∈ {1…6}. Objectives: t₈₀^dose at 38 °C/90% RH; peak device temperature under a 15–42 °C diurnal cycle (1000 W m⁻² peak; organic-capped stacks, hence fixed top emissivity); visible transmittance; a *relative* cost proxy equal to measured deposition time on the same toolset (ALD 0.098 vs parylene 6 nm min⁻¹ [7]; capital/precursor costs and spatial-ALD throughput are outside scope); areal mass; and the fatigue proxy. The analytic evaluator equals the PDE solver at steady state to machine precision and is lag-exact, enabling exhaustive enumeration of 4,104 designs as ground truth. Because three variables permit enumeration, NSGA-II is *not* claimed as a contribution here; a dependency-free implementation (pop. 80, 80 generations, SBX/polynomial) is benchmarked against the exhaustive front to serve as verified machinery for higher-dimensional extensions (per-layer thicknesses, material choice, graded stacks) where enumeration fails.

### 2.5 Exact explainability and number-verified chains
On the full-factorial grid, interventional Shapley values follow exactly from the 2³ coalition group means under the uniform grid measure (efficiency identity ≤7×10⁻¹² of the response), and Sobol shares from exact functional ANOVA under the same measure—no surrogate or sampling approximation (elementary with three features; the point is exactness where exactness is available). Each high-importance driver→objective pair is rendered as a causal chain whose every arrow carries model-internal quantities (f, s, τ², R_in, R_sand, t_lag, σ_c/σ_a) recomputed from probes, with a regression check of the claimed law.

### 2.6 Uncertainty quantification through the calibration pipeline
N = 500 Monte-Carlo draws perturb measured inputs (single-layer series σ = 0.08 decade; digitized anchor 0.10 decade) and placeholder properties (parylene D ×/÷3; S within the validation-constrained 0.5–2×; Ea 30–50 kJ mol⁻¹; pinhole radius ×/÷2; M_crit ×/÷3; σ_c0 ×/÷1.5; fatigue exponent 6–10; rationale table SI S4), re-running the *entire* Stage-A/B calibration per draw so calibration-induced correlations (e.g., f_res anti-correlated with parylene permeability) are preserved. Outputs separate cleanly: regime thresholds are dominated by measurement-noise propagation through the five-point shape fit, whereas absolute lifetime and durability feasibility are prior-dominated (§3.4).

## 3. Results and discussion

### 3.1 Calibrated pinhole physics and a transient, censoring-consistent reading of multilayer WVTR
The fitted f(d) (Fig. 3a) reproduces the non-monotonic single-layer series—closure to a defect floor by ~20 nm, degradation beyond ~50 nm [7]—and its absolute scale implies 5.1 pinholes mm⁻² (444 µm spacing), within Ca-test decoration ranges for good ALD alumina and consistent with defect-dominated transport on coated polymers [18,19]: a plausibility check independent of the fit. Zero-refit *steady-state* prediction of the dyad series fails structurally: the measured improvement is geometric (÷4.7 per dyad, R₃/R₁ ≈ 22 required), whereas the ladder obeys R_sand/R_in = (d_org/d_in)[f + r² ln(s/r)/(2 d_org²)], bounding R₃/R₁ ≤ 11 for all admissible parameters (derivation SI S2)—hence the +0.69-decade 3-dyad overshoot is not a tuning failure but a model-class statement. Simulating the finite ≈72 h run resolves it (Fig. 3b,c): apparent WVTR matches the 2-dyad point to +0.02 decade and the 3-dyad point to −0.24 decade (lag times 74 h and 203 h), remains below the stated instrument limit exactly as the censored data do, and stays within ≤0.4 decade for assumed durations of 2.6–7.2 days (SI Fig. S3). This identifies the multilayer measurements as lag-phase-limited and reconciles apparent geometric scaling with series-resistance physics—an argument available only to a time-domain model [8]. The parylene solubility required for joint agreement (0.5–2 kg m⁻³) coincides with the independent datasheet uptake (<0.1 wt%), a non-circular consistency loop.

### 3.2 Pareto structure and effective dimensionality
Across 4,104 designs the peak device temperature spans 0.10 K and transmittance 0.35 percentage points (organic-capped stacks: ~mK conduction resistance against ~10 W m⁻² K⁻¹ boundary losses; parylene n = 1.639 index-matched to Al₂O₃ n = 1.65). The six-objective problem thus collapses to an effective four-objective one (247 designs on the reduced true front)—itself a design insight: in this material pair, thermal and optical performance are not geometric degrees of freedom and belong to materials selection. The self-implemented NSGA-II lands on the exhaustive front (GD 0.060 mean; the larger inverted GD, 0.168, reflects an 80-point archive approximating a 3,064-point six-objective front—cardinality, not convergence—and is moot in 3-D where the exhaustive front is the deliverable). Lifetime climbs the dyad ladder linearly (+8,150–8,350 h per pair at baseline) with no in-model saturation, so pair count is set by cost/weight constraints; representative designs in Table 2.

### 3.3 Number-verified causal chains, and a correction they forced
Exact Sobol shares (Fig. 5a) rank d_in first for lifetime (0.551), then the d_in×n interaction (0.184)—structural, since the dominant resistance is the product n·R_in(d_in), and physically resonant with the collapse of dyad benefits for particle-rich films [7]: poor barriers cannot be stacked into good ones. Durability loads entirely on d_in (1.00) through σ_c ∝ d^(−1/2), giving N_f ∝ d^(−m/2) (measured log–log slope −3.6 under the fatigue prior). The chain convention proved more than presentational: embedding internals into the n = 1 organic-thickness chain exposed that R_top lies seven orders below R_in, so d_org is inert for single dyads; our earlier reading of the optimizer's boundary choice ("thick organic helps at n = 1") was a tie-breaking artifact, corrected in the published chain. The organic layer contributes to lifetime *only* as the lateral decoupling medium of multilayers (R_sand ∝ 1/d_org for n ≥ 2)—a sign-changing but magnitude-limited effect (Fig. 5c, 6).

### 3.4 Robust design rules under calibration-through uncertainty
Propagating all inputs through re-calibration (Fig. 7): **(G1)** inorganic thickness ≥ 22.5 [21.0–26.0] nm (closure); **(G2)** < 44.0 [37.7–48.0] nm (cracking onset; beyond it lifetime and durability collapse jointly and designs are dominated)—both thresholds measurement-noise-dominated and hence data-anchored; **(G3)** within the window, N_f ∝ d^(−m/2); a 10-year fatigue target needs d_in ≤ 30 [16–49] nm and is unreachable at any thickness in 16% of draws under the fatigue prior—an explicit quantification of the durability proxy's weakness and a target for film-mechanics primaries; **(G4)** the pair law is linear in every draw (R² ≥ 0.9998) with a tight relative gain of 0.5 [0.4–0.5] of the three-dyad lifetime per added dyad, while the absolute gain (7,592 [1,321–45,276] h) is M_crit-prior-dominated: structure settled, absolute lifetime pending dose calibration; **(G5, transport-only and conditional)** absent planarization requirements, transport favors the process-minimum organic thickness (R_sand ∝ 1/d_org); in practice the organic thickness is set by particle planarization [21], a function our model does not resolve—G5 therefore reads: *do not exceed the planarization-required thickness*; **(G6)** thermal/optical objectives delegated to materials selection; **(G7)** thresholds and rankings invariant under M_crit ×/÷3. Fig. 7c condenses these into a fabrication-oriented map.

### 3.5 Limitations and transferability
(i) The d_org optimum sits on the imposed 100 nm bound because pinhole replication/planarization through thin organics is unmodeled [21]; the bound is a process constraint, not physics. (ii) t₈₀^dose is a dose proxy; mapping to photovoltaic T80 and absolute lifetimes (×34 credible span from M_crit) is now floored by a first device-anchored calibration (M_crit^eff >= 0.97 g m-2 from the 7,500 h shelf data [4]; SI S4.4b); a two-sided estimate requires aging beyond the dose threshold. (iii) The cracking onset was calibrated under flexible-substrate handling strain [7]; rigid deployments shift d_crit upward (regime flag). (iv) Durability is a Basquin proxy pending film-mechanics primaries. (v) Calibration and quantitative validation share one source, mitigated by cross-regime prediction, the cross-temperature check [4], solubility consistency, and named falsification targets [3]. (vi) Lifetime ranking is evaluated at 38 °C/90% RH; Arrhenius extrapolation rescales absolute values without reordering geometry. (vii) Activity-linear sorption may break near saturation (water clustering in polymers). (viii) The cost proxy is relative deposition time on one toolset; spatial ALD would change it qualitatively. (ix) The device-side sink is conservative; sub-pinhole accumulation on real devices reduces ingress [17].

## 4. Conclusions
Treating thin-film encapsulation as a geometry problem, a literature-calibrated defect-mediated digital twin—verified against analytic transport; validated zero-refit against multilayer experiments through a transient, censoring-consistent re-reading of sub-detection data; explored to an exhaustively verified Pareto front; explained through exact, number-verified causal chains that corrected our own over-interpretation; and stress-tested by calibration-through uncertainty propagation—yields confidence-qualified design rules, chief among them a 22.5–44 nm inorganic window and a saturation-free linear pair law. The framework is lightweight and released with database and provenance; its consequential open items—the perovskite critical moisture dose and film-mechanics primaries—slot directly into the released pipeline.
